About Monstera Deliciosa

The Monstera Deliciosa, famously known as the Swiss Cheese Plant, is a stunning tropical specimen that brings instant jungle vibes to any space. With its iconic fenestrated leaves, this plant has become a design staple in modern interiors.

Native to the rainforests of Central America, this climbing vine can grow impressively large indoors, creating a dramatic focal point. The natural leaf holes (fenestrations) develop as the plant matures, allowing light to filter down to lower leaves in its natural habitat.

Key Features:

  • • Iconic split leaves with natural fenestrations
  • • Fast-growing climber (up to 6-8 feet indoors)
  • • Air-purifying qualities
  • • Low to moderate care requirements
  • • Perfect for bright, indirect light

Light Requirements

Bright, indirect light is ideal. Avoid direct sunlight which can scorch leaves. Tolerates medium light but fenestrations may not develop fully.

Watering

Water when top 2-3 inches of soil are dry. Typically every 7-10 days. Reduce in winter. Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.

Temperature

Prefers 65-85°F (18-29°C). Avoid temperatures below 55°F (13°C) and cold drafts.

Humidity

Prefers 60%+ humidity. Use a humidifier or pebble tray. Regular misting helps prevent brown tips.

Fertilizer

Feed monthly during spring/summer with balanced liquid fertilizer. Reduce feeding in fall/winter.

Pruning

Prune yellow or damaged leaves at the base. Trim aerial roots if desired. Can be propagated from cuttings.
